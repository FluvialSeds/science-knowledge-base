# Radiocarbon in dissolved methane and dissolved organic carbon in peatland streams

## Overall Scientific Topic

This study uses radiocarbon dating to characterize the age of dissolved methane and dissolved organic carbon in peatland stream systems. The research demonstrates distinct radiocarbon signatures for these carbon forms, revealing different source areas within peat profiles and carbon cycling pathways in peatland ecosystems.

## Methods

Water samples were collected from peatland streams and analyzed for dissolved methane and dissolved organic carbon using radiocarbon (¹⁴C) and stable carbon isotope (δ¹³C) measurements. Samples were processed using standard radiocarbon laboratory protocols to determine both age and isotopic signatures of aquatic carbon pools.

## Results

Analysis revealed significant differences in radiocarbon ages between dissolved methane and dissolved organic carbon, with implications for understanding carbon cycling pathways within peatland ecosystems. The radiocarbon signatures provide diagnostic information about carbon sources and transformation processes.

## Implications

These findings advance understanding of how peatland carbon is mobilized and cycled through aquatic systems, with important implications for predicting carbon emissions and cycling dynamics in peatland-dominated watersheds.
