# Wastewater-boosted biodegradation amplifying seasonal variations of pCO₂ in the Mekong–Tonle Sap river system

## Overall Scientific Topic

Water pollution from untreated wastewater disrupts the ecological integrity of urbanized river systems, yet its impacts on riverine carbon cycling and CO₂ dynamics remain poorly understood in developing countries. This study investigates how wastewater discharge affects organic matter biodegradation and partial pressure of CO₂ (pCO₂) in an urbanized river network, revealing the importance of sewage inputs for carbon turnover in monsoon-influenced tropical systems.

## Methods

Three seasonal field surveys combined with high-resolution measurements and in situ incubation experiments were conducted along the Mekong–Tonle Sap network around Phnom Penh, Cambodia. Dissolved organic carbon (DOC), radiocarbon (Δ¹⁴C), and stable carbon isotope (δ¹³C) measurements were made on water samples. Partial pressure of CO₂ (pCO₂) was measured continuously. In situ incubations tested the biodegradability of riverine organic matter mixed with and without sewage. Excitation-emission matrix (EEM) fluorescence was used to characterize dissolved organic matter composition.

## Results

High-resolution measurements during the dry season revealed large downstream increases in pCO₂ along Mekong reaches receiving Tonle Sap inflows carrying urban sewage, contrasting with minimal spatial variation during monsoon surveys when floodwater reversed flow patterns. During incubations, sewage-supplemented river water showed large nocturnal increases in pCO₂ that exceeded daytime CO₂ uptake by photosynthesis. Sewage addition preferentially stimulated consumption of labile DOC components, indicating enhanced heterotrophic respiration.

## Implications

Wastewater-enhanced biodegradation of riverine organic matter significantly amplifies CO₂ production in urbanized river basins. Neglecting these wastewater-driven processes during long dry periods can lead to substantial underestimation of riverine CO₂ emissions from tropical river systems affected by untreated sewage discharge.
