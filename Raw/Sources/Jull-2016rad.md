# ¹⁴C measurements of dissolved inorganic and organic carbon in Qinghai Lake and inflowing rivers (NE Tibet, Qinghai Plateau), China

## Overall Scientific Topic

This study measures ¹⁴C content of dissolved inorganic and organic carbon in Qinghai Lake and inflowing rivers to understand carbon cycling and establish accurate chronologies for paleoclimate reconstruction. Radiocarbon analysis reveals the main process affecting surface lake DIC is atmospheric exchange, while DOC combines multiple sources, and sediment carbon is explained by mixing of river and groundwater inputs with a complex three-stage ¹⁴C reservoir effect model.

## Methods

Modern samples were collected at Qinghai Lake in October 2003 and October 2009. Radiocarbon measurements were conducted on dissolved inorganic carbon (DIC) and dissolved organic carbon (DOC) samples. Different sampling sites within the lake (Bird Island Dock, Northwest Lake, Qinghai Hotel) and river inflow locations were analyzed. Geochronological analysis incorporated results from sediment trap studies, loss-on-ignition measurements of organic carbon and carbonate content, and previous 210Pb and 137Cs-based sedimentation rate determinations. Measurements of 210Pb and 137Cs activities were used to establish age models for the past 200 years.

## Results

Atmospheric exchange of ¹⁴C emerged as the main process affecting surface dissolved inorganic carbon in the lake. Dissolved organic carbon could be explained as a combination of sources. Sediment carbon could be explained by a model incorporating input from surrounding rivers and groundwater. The three-stage ¹⁴C reservoir offset model provides framework for understanding ¹⁴C chronology of the lake. Results show persistent offsets in ¹⁴C content compared to expected ages on the order of a few hundred to thousands of years, consistent with previous studies but with refined estimates.

## Implications

These direct measurements of ¹⁴C in lake waters improve understanding of the freshwater reservoir effect in this important paleoclimate archive. Accurate correction of radiocarbon dates from Qinghai Lake sediments is essential for precise correlation with other paleoclimate proxies and for reconstructing the history of Asian monsoon variability during the Holocene. The study demonstrates the importance of measuring modern lake systems to establish appropriate ¹⁴C reservoir effect corrections for paleoclimate applications.
