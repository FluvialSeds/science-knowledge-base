# A disordered kinetic model for clumped isotope bond reordering in carbonates


## Overall Scientific Topic

Clumped isotope thermometry (¹³C-¹⁸O covariation in carbonate minerals) provides a powerful tool for reconstructing paleoclimate and paleoenvironmental conditions. However, the interpretation of clumped isotope records requires understanding how isotope compositions change over time through solid-state reordering of bonds at elevated temperatures. This process, known as bond reordering, can alter original paleoclimate signals during burial heating or weathering. This study develops a kinetic model to describe and predict clumped isotope bond reordering in carbonate minerals as a function of temperature and time.

## Methods

The researchers compiled published experimental data on clumped isotope reordering in carbonates from the literature and developed a kinetic model based on transition state theory. The model incorporates activation energy distributions and pre-exponential factors to account for the diversity of bond-reordering pathways and rates. The framework was applied to carbonate samples with known thermal histories to test predictions against observed clumped isotope values and assess the model's ability to reconstruct paleothermometry data.

## Results

A disordered kinetic model successfully describes the temperature and time dependence of clumped isotope bond reordering in carbonates. The model reveals that reordering rates vary significantly depending on mineral phase, composition, and local structural disorder. Activation energies range from approximately 30 to 60 kcal/mol depending on mineralogy, with higher values in more ordered crystal structures. The framework allows quantitative prediction of how original paleoclimate signals are modified by post-burial heating, enabling researchers to evaluate whether measured clumped isotope values preserve primary paleoclimate information or have been partially reset.

## Implications

The disordered kinetic model provides a mechanistic framework for interpreting clumped isotope records in real geological samples with complex thermal histories. Applications include improved paleoclimate reconstructions from sedimentary carbonates, assessment of thermal alteration in buried strata, and optimization of sample selection and analytical approaches for paleoclimate studies. This work advances understanding of isotope kinetics in minerals and improves confidence in paleothermometric reconstructions from clumped isotope data.
