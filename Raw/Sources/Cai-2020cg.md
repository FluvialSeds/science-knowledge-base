# Seasonal variations in strontium and carbon isotope systematics in the Lower Mississippi River: Implications for chemical weathering

## Overall Scientific Topic

Rivers are major conduits for transport of terrestrially-derived carbon and chemical weathering products to marine environments. This study uses multiple isotope systematics incorporating paired carbon isotopes (δ¹³C and Δ¹⁴C), strontium isotopes (⁸⁷Sr/⁸⁶Sr), and water isotopes (δ²H and δ¹⁸O) to investigate coherent relationships among flow paths, chemical weathering regimes, and strontium export fluxes from the Lower Mississippi River.

## Methods

Monthly water samples were collected at a site near Baton Rouge, Louisiana, during 2006–2008 for measurements of water isotopic composition, concentration and isotopic composition of dissolved inorganic carbon (DIC), and concentration and isotopic ratio of strontium along with other major elements. Water temperature, conductivity, and dissolved oxygen were measured in the field. Carbon isotope analyses were conducted on DIC samples using standard mass spectrometry techniques.

## Results

Both δ²H and δ¹⁸O followed similar seasonal patterns with steady increase from a minimum in March to a maximum in July, indicating shift from snowmelt-dominant uppermost Upper Mississippi River during spring to rainfall-induced midcontinent surface runoff and groundwater during other seasons. Values of δ¹³C-DIC ranged from -8.67‰ to -5.96‰ while Δ¹⁴C-DIC varied from -56.8‰ to 27.9‰, corresponding to ¹⁴C ages from contemporary to 415 yr BP. Generally, Δ¹⁴C-DIC increased with increasing δ¹³C-DIC, suggesting variations in bicarbonate sources in response to flow path shifts.

## Implications

Depleted Δ¹⁴C-DIC and δ¹³C-DIC values during wet seasons reflect carbonate mineral dissolution involving soil-derived CO₂, while higher Δ¹⁴C and enriched δ¹³C-DIC values during dry seasons mirror atmospheric CO₂ signatures, implying supply by silicate weathering. Results show that different hydrological flow regimes play unique roles in regulating chemical weathering processes and seasonal variations in isotope systematics and export fluxes of both DIC and strontium from the Mississippi River.
