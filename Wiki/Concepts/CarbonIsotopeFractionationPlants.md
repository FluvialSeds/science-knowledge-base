# Carbon Isotope Fractionation and Plant Photosynthetic Pathways

Stable carbon isotope composition (δ¹³C) of plant-wax lipids reflects the photosynthetic pathway used by plants, providing a powerful tracer for distinguishing vegetation types in sedimentary archives.

## C₃ vs C₄ Fractionation

Different photosynthetic pathways cause differential fractionation of ¹³C:
- **C₃ plants** (grasses, trees, shrubs): More negative δ¹³C values (depleted in ¹³C)
- **C₄ plants** (tropical grasses): Less negative δ¹³C values (enriched in ¹³C)
- **CAM plants**: Intermediate values depending on environmental conditions

Differences between C₃ and C₄ sources can be as large as 13‰, making stable carbon isotopes highly diagnostic for vegetation type.

## Lipid Class Differences

Fractionation differences **between lipid classes** from the same species are negligible (≤1‰) compared to differences between photosynthetic pathways (≤13‰). However, **within a single lipid class**, δ¹³C can vary by up to 6‰ across different chain lengths due to increasing influence of C₄ sources on longer-chain homologues.

This means:
- Different lipid classes from the same plant have similar δ¹³C values
- Different chain lengths within one lipid class may have very different δ¹³C values
- Multiple chain lengths should be measured for robust interpretation

## Secondary Fractionation Effects

While photosynthetic pathway is the primary control on δ¹³C, competing factors can cause secondary effects:
- **Light stress**: Changes fractionation
- **Water stress**: Changes fractionation
- **Nutrient availability**: Minor effects

These secondary effects can complicate interpretation of δ¹³C records, particularly in paleoenvironmental reconstructions where environmental conditions may have differed from modern analogs.

## Advantages for Biomarker Analysis

Using δ¹³C measurements alongside homologue distributions provides complementary constraints:
- δ¹³C identifies photosynthetic pathway
- Molecular distributions show plant functional type effects
- Together, they disambiguate mixing of sources in environmental samples
