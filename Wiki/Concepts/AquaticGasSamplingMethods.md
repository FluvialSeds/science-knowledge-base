# Aquatic Gas Sampling Methods

## Core Concept

Novel field techniques for collecting dissolved gases (CO₂ and CH₄) from freshwater systems for radiocarbon analysis include the coiled membrane vessel (hydrophobic gas-permeable tubing accumulating gases over 4–24 hours), the super headspace method (scaled headspace equilibration with molecular sieve traps), and rapid manual equilibration methods. These techniques enable practical collection in remote, low-concentration environments where traditional methods are impractical.

## Coiled Membrane Vessel (CMV) Method

**Design and operation:**
- 6 meters of hydrophobic, gas-permeable membrane tubing inside 15-liter collapsible water carrier
- 8-liter headspace for gas accumulation
- Water pumped through membrane allows dissolved gas to transfer into headspace
- Collection over 4–24 hours depending on gas concentrations

**Advantages:**
- Reliable collection at low cost in low-concentration environments
- Suitable for collecting both δ¹³CH₄ and ¹⁴CH₄ samples
- Can access remote locations
- Detects unique ¹⁴C signatures from point sources like coal seam gas and landfill gas

**Sample processing:**
- CO₂ removed with soda lime
- CH₄ oxidized to CO₂ at 950°C with platinum catalyst
- Resulting CO₂ analyzed for ¹⁴C and δ¹³C using accelerator mass spectrometry

## Super Headspace Method

**Principle:**
- Scaled-up headspace equilibration for rapid aquatic CO₂ collection
- Particularly suited for low-pH waters
- Uses molecular sieve traps for convenient, stable storage

**Implementation:**
- Modified 5–10 liter Accordion Water Carrier as equilibration chamber
- Two-holed rubber bung with stainless steel pipes and quick-connect couplings
- Chamber collapsed to remove atmospheric air, flushed with CO₂-free air
- Gases injected via syringe into molecular sieve CO₂ traps

**Advantages:**
- High portability and rapid speed
- No special sample preservation requirements
- Captures instantaneous CO₂ samples rather than time-integrated samples
- Enables better capture of individual parts of hydrographic events (storm responses)

## Rapid Manual Equilibration Method

**For methane collection:**
- 1-liter glass airtight container (Kilner jar) with auto-shutoff couplings
- Manual headspace equilibration by shaking to transfer dissolved CH₄ to air
- Infrared gas analyzer determines methane availability
- Field collection in 10–15 minutes per sample

**Practical deployment:**
- Rapid nature makes method practical for sampling across multiple sites
- Enables spatial variability assessment in methane concentrations

## Applications in Arctic and Peatland Systems

These sampling techniques have transformed radiocarbon dating of aquatic gases, enabling:
- **Remote field deployment:** Critical for Arctic and tropical field sites
- **Low-concentration environments:** Successfully collects from waters with minimal dissolved gases
- **Temporal resolution:** Instantaneous sampling captures specific hydrographic moments
- **Multi-site surveys:** Rapid methodology enables comprehensive spatial coverage
