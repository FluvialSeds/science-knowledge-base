# Groundwater Carbon Aging

## Core Concept

Bedrock groundwater in tropical regions contains old dissolved inorganic carbon aged 2,400–4,000 radiocarbon years B.P., often enriched in magmatic CO₂ (3He/4He signatures) from volcanic outgassing. Interbasin groundwater flow transports this ancient carbon into lowland discharge areas where it mixes with young local groundwater, representing a significant old-carbon subsidy to tropical ecosystem carbon cycling.

## Two Groundwater End-Members

**Bedrock groundwater (old, high-solute):**
- Age: 2,400–4,000 radiocarbon years B.P.
- High δ¹³C values (+4.89‰) indicating magmatic CO₂ source
- Very low ¹⁴C activity (7.98 pM, Δ¹⁴C ≈ −1000‰)
- Elevated 3He/4He ratios (6.88) from helium isotope signatures
- Low 36Cl/Cl ratios (17×10⁻¹⁵) indicating magmatic/volcanic sources
- Evidence for weathering of volcanic rock beneath Volcan Barva

**Local groundwater (young, low-solute):**
- Age <55 years (presence of "bomb carbon")
- No magmatic signatures
- Atmospheric sources for helium and chlorine
- Biogenic soil gas CO₂ for dissolved inorganic carbon
- ¹⁴C activity >100 pM (modern)

## Magmatic CO₂ Transport Mechanism

**Source processes:**
- Volcanic outgassing from subterranean magmatic reservoirs
- Dissolution of volcanic rock (weathering of basalt)
- Multi-tracer evidence (3He/4He, 36Cl/Cl, δ¹³C) confirms magmatic origin

**Transport pathway:**
- Interbasin groundwater flow brings bedrock water into lowland discharge area
- High solute content indicates long groundwater residence time
- Mixing with younger local groundwater creates variable intermediate compositions

## Implications for Tropical Carbon Cycling

**Ecosystem-level carbon flux:**
- Significant transport of magmatic CO₂ into lowland tropical rain forest through interbasin flow
- May be substantial relative to other large ecosystem-level carbon fluxes
- Represents old carbon subsidy to plant productivity and soil respiration

**Multi-tracer approach validation:**
- Successfully distinguished groundwater sources
- Validated conceptual hydrologic model
- Demonstrated power of integrating different isotopic systems (¹⁴C, 3He/4He, 36Cl/Cl)

## Methodological Significance

This study demonstrates the value of multiple isotopic tracers for understanding complex groundwater systems. Integration of carbon, helium, and chlorine isotopes enabled:
- Identification of distinct water sources
- Quantification of mixing proportions
- Attribution of carbon sources to magmatic vs biogenic origins
- Understanding of interbasin flow dynamics
